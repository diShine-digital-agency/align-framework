# Frequently asked questions

This page answers common questions about the ALIGN framework. If your question is not covered here, feel free to open an issue or reach out to kevin@dishine.it.

---

## General

### What is the ALIGN framework?

ALIGN is an open-source methodology for building, implementing, and measuring strategic narratives in B2B organizations. The name stands for its five sequential phases: **A**ssess, **L**ink, **I**ntegrate, **G**enerate, **N**avigate. It was developed by [diShine Digital Agency](https://dishine.it) based on real-world client engagements and published research on strategic storytelling.

### Who is this framework for?

Anyone involved in shaping how a B2B company communicates its value — founders, marketing leaders, brand strategists, content teams, and consultants. You do not need a technical background to use it, but experience in marketing strategy or B2B communication will help you get the most out of the templates.

### Is the ALIGN framework free to use?

Yes. The entire repository is released under the [MIT License](../LICENSE). You can use, adapt, and distribute the templates and guides freely. Attribution to diShine Digital Agency is appreciated but not required.

### Do I need to install anything?

No. The framework is entirely documentation-based (markdown files). You can read and fill in the templates using any text editor, GitHub's web interface, or a note-taking app. The [ALIGN Navigator](../tools/align-navigator.html) is a single HTML file that runs in any modern browser — just open it and start.

## Getting started

### Where should I start?

Start with the [step-by-step user guide](how-to-use-this-framework.md). It walks you through every phase, explains who should own each step, and links to the right template at each stage.

If you prefer an interactive experience, open the [ALIGN Navigator](../tools/align-navigator.html) in your browser — it covers all five phases with guided forms, live scoring, and export functionality.

### Do I have to complete every phase?

The framework is designed to be used sequentially — each phase builds on the output of the previous one. That said, if you already have a strong strategic narrative and just need to measure its impact, you can jump directly to Phase 5 (Navigate). The [Narrative Coherence Scorecard](../templates/01-assess-narrative-scorecard.md) is a good diagnostic to determine where you stand.

### Can I use only one template without the rest?

Yes. Each template works as a standalone tool. However, the full value of the framework comes from completing the phases in sequence, since later templates rely on the strategic decisions made in earlier ones.

### How long does it take to go through the full framework?

It depends on the size and complexity of your organization. For a small-to-mid-sized B2B team, a realistic timeline is:

- **Phase 1 (Assess):** 1–2 weeks (scorecard + 5–10 customer interviews).
- **Phase 2 (Link):** 1 week (leadership workshop to build the blueprint).
- **Phase 3 (Integrate):** 1 week (framework selection and element mapping).
- **Phase 4 (Generate):** 2–4 weeks (content production).
- **Phase 5 (Navigate):** Ongoing (measurement and optimization).

## Tools and templates

### What is the ALIGN Navigator?

The ALIGN Navigator is an interactive HTML tool located at `tools/align-navigator.html`. It provides a guided, browser-based experience for all five ALIGN phases. Features include the full Narrative Coherence Scorecard with live scoring, the Strategic Narrative Blueprint worksheet, framework selection diagnostics, a Pixar Pitch builder, the content matrix planner, and a KPI dashboard setup. It auto-saves your work in local storage and can export a complete markdown report.

### Are the templates meant to be filled in directly?

Yes. Each template contains placeholder fields, example values, and scoring tables that you fill in with your own data. You can copy the markdown file, rename it for your engagement, and complete it section by section. The ALIGN Navigator offers the same experience in an interactive format.

### Can I customize the scorecard scoring or add my own dimensions?

Absolutely. The four-dimension, 100-point scorecard is a starting point. If your industry or business model requires additional dimensions (e.g., regulatory messaging compliance), add them. The methodology is more important than the exact number of criteria.

### What formats can I export from the ALIGN Navigator?

The Navigator supports two export formats: a structured markdown report (ready to paste into a document or share with your team) and a JSON backup (for saving and restoring your full session data).

## Framework and methodology

### How is ALIGN different from StoryBrand or the Hero's Journey?

ALIGN is not a storytelling framework in the same sense as StoryBrand or the Hero's Journey. Those are narrative structures — they define how to organize a story. ALIGN is an operational methodology that helps you decide which narrative structure to use, build the strategic foundation behind it, deploy it across channels, and measure its business impact. In Phase 3 (Integrate), you actually select and customize one of those frameworks based on your specific business context.

### Why does the framework emphasize the "villain" so much?

A well-defined villain gives your narrative tension, urgency, and emotional resonance. Without a clear antagonist — an outdated process, a flawed methodology, a systemic industry problem — there is no reason for the hero (your customer) to act. The villain is what moves people from inertia to action.

### Can I use ALIGN for B2C companies?

The framework was designed for B2B contexts, where buying decisions involve multiple stakeholders, longer sales cycles, and higher stakes. Some principles — particularly the narrative coherence assessment and the blueprint — may apply to B2C, but the templates and scoring benchmarks are calibrated for B2B.

### Is the Narrative Coherence Score™ validated by external research?

The scoring methodology is based on principles from behavioral economics, neuroscience, and narrative theory — all referenced in the [technical analysis](narrative-audit-technical-analysis.md) and the README. The score itself is a proprietary diagnostic tool developed by diShine, not an externally validated psychometric instrument. It is designed to be directionally useful, not academically precise.

## Contributing and support

### How can I contribute?

See the [contributing guidelines](../CONTRIBUTING.md) for the full process. In short: fork the repository, create a branch, make your changes, and open a pull request. We welcome improvements to clarity, accuracy, new templates, and translations.

### I found a bug in the ALIGN Navigator. Where do I report it?

Open an issue using the [bug report template](https://github.com/diShine-digital-agency/align-framework/issues/new?template=bug-report.md). Include the browser you are using, steps to reproduce the problem, and what you expected to happen.

### Can I use the ALIGN framework with my own clients?

Yes. The MIT License allows commercial use. You can use the templates in consulting engagements, adapt them for your methodology, and distribute them to clients. Mentioning the source is appreciated but not legally required.
