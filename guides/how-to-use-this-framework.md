# How to use the ALIGN framework: a step-by-step guide

**Version:** 1.2.0

The ALIGN framework is not a collection of isolated writing exercises; it is a sequential, operational engine designed to move a B2B company from "feature-led marketing" to "strategic narrative alignment." 

This guide explains the exact sequence in which to deploy these tools, why each step matters, and who should own it. It is designed for both technical founders and marketing leaders — if any term is unfamiliar, check the [glossary](../GLOSSARY.md).

> **Prefer a guided experience?** The [ALIGN Navigator](../tools/align-navigator.html) covers the same sequence below in an interactive browser-based format with live scoring, form fields, and one-click export. You can use it alongside these templates or on its own.

---

## Phase 1: Assess (the reality check)

Before you can build a new narrative, you must objectively measure how broken the current one is. This phase moves the conversation from subjective opinions ("I don't like our website copy") to quantified data.

### Step 1.1: The internal audit
*   **Tool:** [`templates/01-assess-narrative-scorecard.md`](../templates/01-assess-narrative-scorecard.md) (or the Assess section of the [ALIGN Navigator](../tools/align-navigator.html))
*   **Owner:** Head of Marketing or Founder.
*   **Why it matters:** You cannot fix what you cannot measure. This 100-point scorecard forces you to evaluate your current messaging across four dimensions (Narrative Consistency, Message Hierarchy, Emotional Resonance, Competitive Differentiation). 
*   **Action:** Score your current website, primary sales deck, and top three case studies. If you score below 60, your narrative is actively costing you revenue.

### Step 1.2: The external validation
*   **Tool:** [`templates/narrative-audit-interview-guide.md`](../templates/narrative-audit-interview-guide.md)
*   **Reference:** [`guides/narrative-audit-technical-analysis.md`](narrative-audit-technical-analysis.md)
*   **Owner:** Product Marketing or External Consultant.
*   **Why it matters:** Internal teams suffer from the "Curse of Knowledge" — they know too much about the product to explain it simply. You must validate your scorecard against market reality.
*   **Action:** Conduct 5-10 interviews with recent wins and losses using the 20-question guide. The technical analysis document explains the psychological principles (like Status Quo Bias and Loss Aversion) behind each question so you know exactly what to listen for.

---

## Phase 2: Link (the strategic foundation)

This is the most critical phase. Do not skip to writing copy until this blueprint is approved by the executive team.

### Step 2.1: Build the blueprint
*   **Tool:** [`templates/02-link-narrative-blueprint.md`](../templates/02-link-narrative-blueprint.md) (or the Link section of the [ALIGN Navigator](../tools/align-navigator.html))
*   **Owner:** Executive Leadership Team (CEO, CRO, CMO).
*   **Why it matters:** A narrative only works if it is inextricably linked to your business strategy. The blueprint forces alignment on the fundamental transformation you provide: the Before State, the After State, the Villain (the root cause of the customer's pain), and your role as the Guide.
*   **Action:** Complete the blueprint in a dedicated leadership workshop. It must be a consensus document. If sales and marketing disagree on the Villain, the narrative will fail in the market.

---

## Phase 3: Integrate (the structural skeleton)

With the strategic foundation set, you must now choose the architectural structure that will carry your message.

### Step 3.1: Select the framework
*   **Tool:** [`templates/03-integrate-framework-selection.md`](../templates/03-integrate-framework-selection.md) (Part 1) (or the Integrate section of the [ALIGN Navigator](../tools/align-navigator.html))
*   **Reference:** [`guides/framework-comparison-matrix.md`](framework-comparison-matrix.md)
*   **Owner:** Head of Content or Brand Strategist.
*   **Why it matters:** Different sales motions require different narrative structures. A transactional SaaS product needs the clarity of StoryBrand; a complex enterprise platform requires the transformational arc of the Hero's Journey.
*   **Action:** Use the 5-dimension scoring matrix to determine your ideal framework (StoryBrand, Hero's Journey, or a Hybrid).

### Step 3.2: Map the elements
*   **Tool:** [`templates/03-integrate-framework-selection.md`](../templates/03-integrate-framework-selection.md) (Part 2 & 3)
*   **Owner:** Content Team.
*   **Why it matters:** You must translate the high-level blueprint from Phase 2 into the specific structural beats of your chosen framework.
*   **Action:** Fill out the specific mapping worksheet for your chosen framework, then synthesize the entire narrative into a 6-sentence "Pixar Pitch" that any employee can memorize.

---

## Phase 4: Generate (the execution matrix)

A narrative is useless if it only lives in a strategy document. It must be deployed across every touchpoint.

### Step 4.1: Map content to the buyer journey
*   **Tool:** [`templates/04-generate-content-matrix.md`](../templates/04-generate-content-matrix.md) (or the Generate section of the [ALIGN Navigator](../tools/align-navigator.html))
*   **Owner:** Demand Generation & Content Marketing.
*   **Why it matters:** Buyers need different parts of the story at different times. Top-of-funnel content must focus on the Villain and the Before State; bottom-of-funnel content must focus on the Guide's authority and the After State.
*   **Action:** Map your core messages to specific channels (LinkedIn, SEO, Email) and journey stages (Awareness, Consideration, Decision). This becomes your editorial calendar.

---

## Phase 5: Navigate (the feedback loop)

Storytelling is not a creative art; it is a measurable business process.

### Step 5.1: Track the impact
*   **Tool:** [`templates/05-navigate-measurement-dashboard.md`](../templates/05-navigate-measurement-dashboard.md) (or the Navigate section of the [ALIGN Navigator](../tools/align-navigator.html))
*   **Owner:** Marketing Operations / RevOps.
*   **Why it matters:** If you cannot prove that the new narrative is driving revenue, you will lose executive support.
*   **Action:** Implement the 9-metric KPI dashboard. Track leading indicators (Time on Site, Scroll Depth) and lagging indicators (Sales Cycle Velocity, Win Rate). Use the A/B testing guidance to continuously optimize the message.
