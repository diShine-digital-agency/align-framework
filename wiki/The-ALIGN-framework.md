# The ALIGN framework

The ALIGN framework is an open-source operational methodology developed by [diShine Digital Agency](https://dishine.it) for diagnosing narrative disconnects and implementing strategic storytelling across B2B digital ecosystems. ALIGN stands for five sequential phases: **Assess**, **Link**, **Integrate**, **Generate**, **Navigate**.

This page explains what ALIGN is, the problem it addresses, how it differs from storytelling frameworks, and how its five phases connect.

---

## What ALIGN is

ALIGN is not a storytelling framework. It is an operational methodology that helps B2B companies build, implement, and measure strategic narratives.

Where storytelling frameworks define how to structure a story, ALIGN provides the process for deciding which structure to use, building the strategic foundation behind it, deploying it across channels, and measuring its business impact. The methodology was developed by diShine Digital Agency based on real-world client engagements and published research, first introduced through [diShine's research on strategic storytelling](https://dishine.it/blog/strategic-storytelling-to-improve-brand-identity-and-get-revenue-growth/).

ALIGN treats storytelling as a core business capability rather than a marketing tactic. It produces measurable deliverables at every phase: a quantitative baseline score, a strategic blueprint, a framework selection decision, a content deployment plan, and a KPI dashboard tied to revenue outcomes.

The full repository — including all templates, guides, and the interactive tool — is available at the [ALIGN framework repository](https://github.com/diShine-digital-agency/align-framework).

---

## The problem ALIGN solves

Most B2B companies lead with features instead of narratives. Product pages list technical specifications. Sales decks stack feature comparisons. Marketing content describes capabilities without connecting them to the customer's transformation journey.

This is not a content problem; it is a narrative architecture problem. When storytelling is disconnected from business strategy, it becomes a creative exercise with no measurable business impact.

Two data points illustrate the scale of this gap:

- **Revenue impact of narrative alignment.** McKinsey found that companies aligning their narrative architecture with strategic priorities achieve 18% higher revenue growth than those with disconnected approaches.
- **Measurement gap.** A 2023 Forrester study found that only 23% of B2B marketers can effectively measure the impact of their narrative approach on sales performance.

ALIGN addresses both sides of this problem. It provides a structured, repeatable process for building strategic narratives (closing the alignment gap) and includes a dedicated measurement phase with defined KPIs (closing the measurement gap).

## How ALIGN differs from storytelling frameworks

Storytelling frameworks — such as the Hero's Journey, StoryBrand, or PAS (Problem–Agitation–Solution) — define how to organize a story. They provide narrative structures: character roles, plot sequences, and rhetorical patterns. They answer the question "how should we tell this story?"

ALIGN operates at a different level. It answers the questions that come before and after that:

- **Before:** What is our current narrative state? Where are the gaps? What does the customer actually experience? Which business objectives should the narrative serve?
- **During:** Which storytelling framework fits our context? How do we customize it? How do we synthesize it into a usable narrative architecture?
- **After:** How do we deploy the narrative across channels? How do we align teams around it? How do we measure whether it is working?

In practice, ALIGN and storytelling frameworks are complementary. During [Phase 3: Integrate](Phase-3-Integrate), you select and customize one or more storytelling frameworks (Hero's Journey, StoryBrand, PAS, Pixar Pitch, JTBD) based on a structured scoring process. The framework you choose becomes the narrative structure inside ALIGN's broader operational process.

For a detailed comparison of storytelling frameworks and when each applies, see [Storytelling frameworks compared](Storytelling-frameworks-compared).

## The five phases

ALIGN follows a strict sequential order. Each phase produces a defined output that feeds into the next. Skipping phases or working out of sequence undermines the methodology's effectiveness.

![ALIGN Framework Overview](https://github.com/diShine-digital-agency/align-framework/blob/main/assets/align-framework-overview.png?raw=true)

### A — Assess: narrative ecosystem mapping

Map narrative gaps across all digital touchpoints. Identify story fragmentation, measure emotional impact, and evaluate competitive differentiation to establish a quantitative baseline.

This phase includes customer validation: a set of structured interviews with recent wins and losses, using a [20-question guide](https://github.com/diShine-digital-agency/align-framework/blob/main/templates/narrative-audit-interview-guide.md) grounded in behavioral economics principles (Status Quo Bias, Loss Aversion, Anchoring, Curse of Knowledge, Peak-End Rule).

**Output:** Narrative Coherence Score™ (0–100), quantifying the alignment between current storytelling and the ideal state across four dimensions: narrative consistency, message hierarchy, emotional resonance, and competitive differentiation.

**Key deliverables:**

- Narrative Coherence Scorecard — the quantitative assessment template.
- Customer validation interview findings — synthesized themes from structured interviews.
- Gap analysis — specific areas where the current narrative breaks down.

→ Full details: [Phase 1: Assess](Phase-1-Assess)

---

### L — Link: strategic-narrative alignment

Align business strategy with storytelling architecture. Connect strategic priorities to narrative elements so that every story component serves defined business objectives and verified customer needs.

This phase produces the foundational strategic document that dictates what you say, why you say it, and how it positions you in the market.

**Output:** Strategic Narrative Blueprint™ — defines the customer transformation journey (before state → after state), the villain (the root cause of the customer's problem), the guide (your brand's role), and the unique mechanism (your differentiated approach to solving the problem).

**Key deliverables:**

- Customer transformation journey map.
- Villain definition and root cause analysis.
- Guide positioning (empathy and authority statements).
- Unique mechanism articulation.
- Strategic narrative statement.

→ Full details: [Phase 2: Link](Phase-2-Link)

---

### I — Integrate: framework selection and customization

Select and customize the optimal storytelling framework based on your specific business context, audience, and strategic objectives. This is the phase where you choose a narrative structure — not before.

The selection uses a structured scoring process that evaluates five frameworks against your context:

- **Hero's Journey** — best for complex transformations with extended sales cycles.
- **StoryBrand** — best for clear positioning and message simplification.
- **PAS (Problem–Agitation–Solution)** — best for direct response and conversion-focused content.
- **Pixar Pitch** — best for pitch decks and concise stakeholder communication.
- **JTBD (Jobs to Be Done)** — best for product-led narratives focused on functional outcomes.

**Output:** Framework selection decision, customized narrative architecture mapping your strategic elements to the chosen framework's structure, and a Pixar Pitch synthesis (a one-sentence narrative that captures the full transformation story).

**Key deliverables:**

- Framework scoring matrix with weighted criteria.
- Narrative architecture — before/villain/guide/after model.
- Element mapping — strategic blueprint components mapped to framework-specific roles.
- Pixar Pitch — "Once upon a time... Every day... One day... Because of that... Until finally..."

→ Full details: [Phase 3: Integrate](Phase-3-Integrate)

---

### G — Generate: content ecosystem development

Produce strategic content assets mapped to specific channels, formats, and buyer journey stages. Adapt core story elements across the content ecosystem while maintaining narrative consistency.

This phase translates the narrative architecture from Phase 3 into a concrete content plan, ensuring that the right part of the story reaches the right audience at the right time through the right channel.

**Output:** Content Narrative Matrix — a structured planning document that maps core messages to content formats (blog posts, case studies, landing pages, email sequences, social posts, sales collateral) across buyer journey stages (awareness, consideration, decision, retention).

**Key deliverables:**

- Channel-specific message adaptations.
- Journey-stage content mapping.
- Content brief templates aligned to the narrative architecture.
- Editorial calendar integration points.

→ Full details: [Phase 4: Generate](Phase-4-Generate)

---

### N — Navigate: implementation and optimization

Roll out the strategic narrative across all channels, align internal teams around the narrative, track performance metrics, and continuously refine based on market feedback and business outcomes.

This phase connects storytelling activity to business results through a defined set of KPIs.

**Output:** Narrative KPI Dashboard with 9 metrics organized across three tiers:

- **Narrative metrics** — message pull-through rate, narrative consistency score, content engagement depth.
- **Business metrics** — sales cycle velocity, win rate change, customer acquisition cost.
- **Alignment metrics** — team narrative adoption, cross-channel consistency, customer story recall.

**Key deliverables:**

- KPI dashboard with baseline and target values.
- Team alignment plan and narrative training materials.
- Review cadence and optimization triggers.
- Feedback loop connecting market response to narrative refinement.

→ Full details: [Phase 5: Navigate](Phase-5-Navigate)

## Design principles

The ALIGN framework follows a set of deliberate architectural choices:

- **Technology-light.** Templates are plain markdown files. The interactive tool ([ALIGN Navigator](https://github.com/diShine-digital-agency/align-framework/blob/main/tools/align-navigator.html)) is a single HTML file with no external dependencies, no build system, and no server requirement. It runs in any modern browser.
- **Sequential and cumulative.** Each phase builds on the output of the previous one. The methodology does not support cherry-picking individual phases out of context.
- **Output-driven.** Every phase produces a named, defined deliverable. Progress is measured by deliverable completion, not by activity volume.
- **Strategy-first.** Narrative structure selection (Phase 3) happens only after strategic alignment (Phases 1–2). This prevents the common mistake of choosing a storytelling framework before understanding what the narrative needs to accomplish.
- **Measurement-integrated.** KPI definition is not an afterthought; it is a dedicated phase with its own deliverables and review cadence.

For a full description of the repository structure and tooling, see [Repository structure and tools](Repository-structure-and-tools).

## Who ALIGN is for

The methodology is designed for professionals working in B2B contexts where narrative coherence directly affects business outcomes:

- **Founders and CEOs** — defining the company narrative and ensuring it aligns with strategic direction.
- **Marketing leaders** — building a narrative-driven content strategy with measurable impact.
- **Brand strategists** — diagnosing narrative disconnects and designing coherent brand stories.
- **Content teams** — producing content that follows a strategic narrative architecture rather than ad hoc editorial decisions.
- **Consultants and agencies** — using a structured methodology for narrative-driven client engagements.

The framework assumes familiarity with B2B marketing and strategic communication concepts. For definitions of key terms, see the [Glossary](Glossary).
