# Glossary

This glossary defines the key terms used throughout the ALIGN framework. It is written for a mixed audience — whether you come from a marketing, strategy, or technical background, these definitions should give you a clear baseline.

---

## Framework terms

**ALIGN.**
An acronym for the five sequential phases of the framework: **A**ssess, **L**ink, **I**ntegrate, **G**enerate, **N**avigate. Each phase builds on the output of the previous one.

**Narrative Coherence Score™.**
A quantitative score (0–100) produced by the Phase 1 scorecard. It measures how well your current storytelling holds together across four dimensions: narrative consistency, message hierarchy, emotional resonance, and competitive differentiation.

**Strategic Narrative Blueprint™.**
The foundational document produced in Phase 2. It defines the customer transformation journey (before state → after state), the villain, the guide (your brand), and the unique mechanism — all tied directly to your business strategy.

**Content Narrative Matrix.**
A planning tool used in Phase 4 that maps your core messages to specific content formats, channels, and buyer journey stages. It ensures that the right part of your story reaches the right audience at the right time.

**Narrative KPI Dashboard.**
A measurement framework used in Phase 5 that connects storytelling metrics (message pull-through rate, content engagement depth) to business outcomes (sales cycle velocity, win rate, customer acquisition cost).

**ALIGN Navigator.**
A self-contained, interactive HTML tool (`tools/align-navigator.html`) that walks you through all five phases in a browser. It includes live scoring, form fields, local data storage, and one-click markdown export. No installation required.

## Storytelling and narrative terms

**The hero.**
In strategic storytelling, the hero is always the customer — not your product or brand. The entire narrative is structured around their journey from a painful status quo to a successful outcome.

**The villain.**
The root cause of the hero's problem. In B2B contexts, the villain is rarely a direct competitor; it is typically an outdated methodology, a broken process, or a systemic industry flaw that keeps the customer stuck.

**The guide.**
Your brand's role in the narrative. The guide demonstrates empathy (understanding the hero's pain) and authority (proving the ability to solve it). Think of it as the mentor archetype — Yoda, not Luke Skywalker.

**The unique mechanism.**
The specific, defensible way you solve the hero's problem. It is the bridge between the "before" state and the "after" state, and it differentiates your approach from alternatives in the market.

**Before state / after state.**
The two ends of the customer transformation. The "before" state describes the hero's current painful reality across three levels: external (functional), internal (emotional), and philosophical (moral). The "after" state mirrors these same levels with the successful outcome.

**Pixar Pitch.**
A six-sentence narrative synthesis exercise ("Once upon a time… Every day… One day… Because of that… Because of that… Until finally…") used to distill your entire strategic narrative into a format any team member can memorize and repeat.

## Storytelling frameworks referenced

**Hero's Journey.**
A 12-stage narrative structure originally described by Joseph Campbell and adapted by Christopher Vogler. It follows the hero through departure, initiation, and return. In B2B, it is best suited for complex enterprise sales with long decision cycles.

**StoryBrand.**
A 7-part framework developed by Donald Miller that focuses on extreme clarity: a character has a problem, meets a guide, receives a plan, takes action, avoids failure, and achieves success. Best suited for website messaging and brand positioning.

**PAS (Problem–Agitate–Solution).**
A classic copywriting formula that identifies a specific pain point, intensifies its emotional and financial consequences, and then presents the solution. Commonly used in cold outreach and landing pages.

**JTBD (Jobs-to-be-Done).**
A product-centric narrative framework that focuses on the "job" the customer is trying to accomplish, rather than on demographics or features. Structured as: "When I… I want to… So I can…"

## Business and marketing terms

**B2B (Business-to-Business).**
Commercial transactions or relationships between companies, as opposed to between a company and individual consumers (B2C). The ALIGN framework is designed specifically for B2B contexts.

**Go-to-market (GTM).**
The strategy and set of actions a company uses to bring a product or service to market. Includes positioning, messaging, channel selection, and sales enablement.

**MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead).**
A lead that has been identified by the marketing team as more likely to become a customer based on engagement signals (e.g., downloading a whitepaper, attending a webinar).

**Sales cycle velocity.**
The average time it takes to move a prospect from initial qualification (MQL) to a closed deal. Strategic narrative alignment typically shortens this by reducing confusion and building trust earlier.

**Win rate.**
The percentage of competitive deals that result in a sale. A clear, differentiated narrative improves win rates by making it easier for internal champions to advocate for your solution.

**NRR (Net Revenue Retention).**
The percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers over a given period, including expansions, contractions, and churn. A strong "after state" vision drives expansion revenue.

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost).**
The total cost of marketing and sales efforts divided by the number of new customers acquired. Better storytelling lowers CAC by improving conversion rates across the funnel.

**NPS (Net Promoter Score).**
A metric that measures customer loyalty by asking how likely they are to recommend your product or service to others. Narrative-driven customer experiences tend to produce higher NPS scores.

## Technical terms used in the repository

**Markdown.**
A lightweight text formatting language used for all templates and guides in this repository. Files ending in `.md` are markdown files and can be read in any text editor or rendered in GitHub, VS Code, or similar tools.

**HTML.**
HyperText Markup Language — the standard language for web pages. The ALIGN Navigator (`tools/align-navigator.html`) is a single HTML file that runs entirely in the browser with no server or installation.

**Local storage.**
A browser feature that allows a web page to save data on the user's device. The ALIGN Navigator uses local storage to auto-save your progress so you can close the browser and resume later.

**D2 / Mermaid (.d2 / .mmd).**
Diagram-as-code formats used for the source files of the framework diagrams in the `assets/` folder. The `.png` images are rendered from these source files. D2 and Mermaid allow diagrams to be version-controlled alongside the documentation.
