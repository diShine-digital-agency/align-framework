# Phase 5: NAVIGATE — Narrative Measurement Dashboard

**Version:** 1.1.0

The final phase of the ALIGN Framework involves rolling out your strategic narrative across channels and measuring its impact.

Many organizations struggle to measure the business impact of their storytelling efforts. Without clear metrics connecting narrative effectiveness to business outcomes, strategic storytelling remains vulnerable to budget cuts. This dashboard provides a practical framework for tracking narrative KPIs.

---

## How to Use This Dashboard

1.  **Establish Baselines:** Before launching your new strategic narrative, record your current performance across these metrics.
2.  **Set Targets:** Define realistic goals based on industry benchmarks and your specific business context.
3.  **Track Monthly/Quarterly:** Monitor these metrics regularly to identify areas where the narrative is resonating and where it needs refinement.
4.  **A/B Test the Story:** Don't just test button colors; test different narrative angles (e.g., focusing on the "Villain" vs. the "Transformation").

## The Narrative KPI Dashboard

| Metric Category | Specific Metric | Formula / How to Measure | Target Benchmark (B2B SaaS) | Tool to Measure |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Narrative Consistency** | **Message Pull-Through Rate** | % of sales calls where reps use the core narrative framework (e.g., identifying the "Villain"). | > 70% | Gong, Chorus.ai, Call Reviews |

| | **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** | % of recurring revenue retained from existing customers (strong "After" state vision drives expansion). | > 110% | Billing/Subscription Software |

## A/B Testing the Narrative (Not Just Copy)

To optimize your storytelling, you must test the structural elements of your narrative, not just the words used to express them.

*   **Test the Villain:** Does focusing on "Wasted Marketing Spend" perform better than "Inaccurate Attribution Data"?
*   **Test the Hero's Journey Stage:** Does top-of-funnel content perform better when focused on the "Call to Adventure" (the trigger event) or the "Ordinary World" (the painful status quo)?
*   **Test the Guide's Authority:** Do case studies perform better when they highlight your proprietary methodology (The Mechanism) or your deep industry experience (Empathy)?
