# Narrative Audit: Customer Validation Interview Guide

**Version:** 1.0.2

The most dangerous assumption in B2B marketing is that your internal narrative matches your customer's external reality. The Narrative Audit is a structured qualitative research methodology designed to validate your Strategic Narrative Blueprint (Phase 2) against actual market perception.

This is not a standard customer satisfaction (CSAT) survey or a feature request interview. The goal is to uncover the cognitive biases, emotional triggers, and internal political dynamics that drove the purchase decision.

---

## Interview Methodology & Best Practices

1.  **Target Audience:** Interview 5-7 recent wins (closed within 90 days) and 3-5 recent losses. Do not interview legacy customers who bought 3 years ago; their buying context is outdated.
2.  **The "Five Whys" Rule:** Never accept the first functional answer. If they say "we bought because of the API," ask *why* the API mattered, *who* was demanding it, and *what* would have happened if they didn't get it.
3.  **Silence is a Tool:** After asking a question, wait. Let them fill the silence. The most valuable narrative insights often come after a 3-second pause.

---

## Section 1: The Status Quo & The Trigger (The "Before" State)

*Objective: Understand the exact conditions that forced them out of inertia. What was the true cost of doing nothing?*

**1. Take me back to the day you realized you had to fix this problem. What exactly happened?**
*   *Analysis Note:* You are looking for the "Call to Adventure." Was it an internal failure, a new executive mandate, or a market shift? This becomes the opening hook of your narrative.

**2. Before you started looking for a solution, how were you handling this internally?**
*   *Analysis Note:* This defines your true competitor (often Excel, a legacy system, or an intern). Your narrative must position against *this*, not just other software vendors.

**3. What was the most frustrating part of that old process? How did it impact your day-to-day work?**
*   *Analysis Note:* This uncovers the "Internal Problem" (the emotional toll). Listen for words like "anxiety," "wasted time," or "embarrassing."

**4. If you had done nothing and kept the old process for another year, what would have been the business impact?**
*   *Analysis Note:* This quantifies the "Cost of Inaction." If they struggle to answer this, your narrative lacks urgency.

**5. Who else in the organization was feeling the pain of the old process?**
*   *Analysis Note:* Identifies secondary personas and potential internal champions.

---

## Section 2: The Search & The Villain (The Evaluation Phase)

*Objective: Understand how they framed the problem in their own minds and what they perceived as the root cause (The Villain).*

**6. When you first started Googling or asking peers for advice, what exact words or phrases did you use?**
*   *Analysis Note:* This is pure SEO and copywriting gold. Use their exact vocabulary in your top-of-funnel messaging.

**7. What was the fundamental flaw in the other solutions you evaluated? Why didn't they work for you?**
*   *Analysis Note:* This helps define the "Villain" (the outdated methodology or philosophical flaw of your competitors).

**8. Was there a specific moment during your research where your understanding of the problem completely changed?**
*   *Analysis Note:* You are looking for a paradigm shift. If your content caused this shift, your narrative is working.

**9. What was the biggest risk or fear you had about choosing a new vendor in this space?**
*   *Analysis Note:* This highlights the "Refusal of the Call." Your narrative must preemptively dismantle this specific fear.

**10. How did you explain the problem to your CFO or the person holding the budget?**
*   *Analysis Note:* This reveals the "Philosophical Problem" translated into business terms. It shows you how to arm your Champion.

---

## Section 3: The Guide & The Unique Mechanism (The Decision)

*Objective: Validate your brand positioning and the perceived value of your proprietary approach.*

**11. When you first encountered our brand, what was your initial impression?**
*   *Analysis Note:* Tests the clarity of your top-of-funnel messaging (Phase 1: Assess).

**12. What was the specific feature, capability, or concept that made you say, "Yes, this is the one"?**
*   *Analysis Note:* Validates your "Unique Mechanism." Is it what you *think* your differentiator is?

**13. How would you describe what we do to a colleague in one sentence?**
*   *Analysis Note:* This is the ultimate test of your Pixar Pitch (Phase 3). If 5 customers give 5 completely different answers, your narrative is fragmented.

**14. Did you feel like we understood your specific industry challenges better than the others? Why?**
*   *Analysis Note:* Validates the "Empathy" component of your Guide persona.

**15. What piece of content, case study, or interaction gave you the confidence that we could actually deliver?**
*   *Analysis Note:* Validates the "Authority" component of your Guide persona.

---

## Section 4: The Transformation & The After State (The ROI)

*Objective: Quantify the functional and emotional success to build the "After" state of your blueprint.*

**16. Now that you are fully implemented, what is the biggest functional change in your day-to-day operations?**
*   *Analysis Note:* Validates the "External Success." Look for measurable metrics (time saved, revenue gained).

**17. How has this impacted your personal role or reputation within the company?**
*   *Analysis Note:* Validates the "Internal Success." Did you make the Champion look like a hero?

**18. What is something you can do now that was completely impossible six months ago?**
*   *Analysis Note:* This defines the "Philosophical Success" — the new reality you have created for them.

**19. If you were to leave your current company and go to a competitor, would you bring us with you? Why?**
*   *Analysis Note:* The ultimate test of narrative loyalty and the "Return with the Elixir" stage.

**20. Is there anything we *didn't* ask about your buying journey that you think is important for us to know?**
*   *Analysis Note:* The catch-all question that often yields the most surprising insights.

---

## Post-Interview Synthesis

After completing 5-10 interviews, map the recurring themes directly back into your [Strategic Narrative Blueprint](02-link-narrative-blueprint.md).

*   If the customers' words do not match your blueprint, **change the blueprint.**
*   If the customers' perceived value does not match your marketing copy, **change the copy.**
*   The market's reality is the only reality that matters.
